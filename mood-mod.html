moodmod

Objective

Cost and access of resources are barriers to users who want to manage their mental health.

My Role

In this project I was responsible for conducting user interviews to achieve insight into our users’ pain points. I was accountable for creating both our users’ journey and storyboard as well as generating our initial sketches, and reiterating them with user feedback.

My next step was creating a digital wireframe based on insights from the paper prototype. After a series of iterations gathering user input and generating wireframes, I created a high fidelity prototype ready for delivery.

Research

User interview
Affinity mapping

Insight

Our design decisions were driven by our research which consisted of user interviews, Affinity mapping, a competitive analysis, and secondary data. Our key findings were:

  • * 1 in 5 adults have a mental health condition. That is over 40 millions americans.
  • * 56% of american adults with a mental health condition do not receive treatment
  • * 51% of Americans said that mental healthcare costs are simply too high.

Solution

We believe that our mood monitoring app will be of significant help to the majority of Americans that struggle with mental health conditions. Users of this app will be able to seamlessly evaluate and monitor their mood over time. Using real time mood data, this app will provide instant recommendations to improve users’ mood. Though it is not a direct replacement for a professional medical service, we believe that by promoting mental well-being our app will help significantly reduce the need for doctor’s office visits.

User Goal

People who knowingly live with mental issues throughout their daily lives and understand that they need to change for the better, but unable to do so due to a variety of reasons, such as lack of health insurance or time away from work. Our app is not meant to be of any use for people with severe mental conditions, who are unable to help themselves. However, people with mild mental issues who are willing to help themselves will greatly benefit from our app.

BOB'S JOURNEY

In the storyboard I wanted to focus on my users’ pain points and how my app will make an instant and positive impact to my users. Whenever my users sign in and evaluate their mood, my app should immediately return a result that includes recommendation, whether it is a simple suggestion to go for a jog or connecting with a specialist. Users of this app should be able to freely track and monitor their mood over time, so they can improve their mental health.

Sketches

After our research and brainstorming sessions with my team members, I was in a position to start putting together everything on a piece of paper using pencil and pen. I did my initial sketch and shared it with my team members. Using their input back and forth I modified my sketch a few times. In my final sketch I made sure that my users have a pleasant experience when they visit my site. I wanted to be sure that my users have easy access and a clear path to what needs to be done next.

Prototype

After making sure that my sketches and paper prototypes were satisfactory, the next step was to work on digital wireframes using Figma. I have used all the aspects of the user flow, design and sketches to facilitate better understanding of my prototype structure.

Next Steps

Make the app adaptive or responsive for web and iOS.
Develop a customized notification system to prompt users to complete surveys on a regular basis.
Develop a comprehensive directory of mental health specialists.

Conclusion

After working collectively with my team members, sharing knowledge with each other and examining user input, I realized what is most important is putting myself in my users’ position before working on a solution. Understanding my users’ pain points and addressing them will help me create a better, functional application that will benefit both parties.
