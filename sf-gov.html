My Role

Based on the content and research from SFDPH and SF Health Network. My job is to create a "Gender Health SF" page to the new website following the SF.Gov Style guide. Migrate all the valid content from SFDPH to SF.GOV by following an update Information Architecture. Create a few separated sections at SF.GOV such us: "Staff and Surgeon", "Upcoming events and training" and "Community and patient resources".

Competitive Insight

Insight from research "Patient and Provider" divided into each individual section. Each section contains information such as. Surgery Preparation, Fertility Planning, FAQ, Training, Pre-surgical Assessments, Surgical Info for Patients, Forms and Resources.

“Calendar of Events” is the new page that added to both SF health network and SFGov.org. User can find the “Upcoming Events, Training” and “Orientation Videos”.

knowing your “Staff and Surgeons” is also important. In this page, user can view their doctor profile and their speciality.

Storyboard

In this storyboard, I highlight Jordan journey searching for feminizing mammoplasty through the SFDPH website before and after. I show Jordan has a better experience from the new redesign website. In the new site, Jordan finds everything she needs step by step.

Jordan needs a feminizing mammoplasty.
She visits SFDPH website, looking for the process, requirement, assessment or support group that she can access or reach out. She left the site without finding what she needs.
Jordan be able to find the resources that she needs throughout the new SFDPH website and be able to schedule her surgery.
Jordan feels happy and joyful, having feminized features to affirm her gender identity is a dream come true.

Conclusion

Took us a while to understand how the surgical process works due to massive content and form. After the second meeting with clients with additional resources and information, we gain a better understanding of how Gender Health SF works.

When we showed our wireframe, we found out that our clients were not ready to be found quickly or stay side by side next to “Get transgender community” due of the resources (make sense).

I believe we have done a great job redesign “Transgender Health” page from SF health network site and implement “Transgender Health” page to SFGov.org. It is much easier to navigate. Each site keeps its own color scheme and style guide.
